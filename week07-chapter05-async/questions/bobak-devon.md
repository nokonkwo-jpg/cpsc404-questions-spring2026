## Question

Chapter 5 frames workplace relationships like an iterated Prisoner’s Dilemma, where trust is built through repeated cooperation (often modeled as Tit-for-Tat). But in real teams, people make “defections” for reasons that aren’t purely selfish—deadline pressure, unclear instructions, or conflicting incentives.

How should you adapt a Tit-for-Tat strategy when you can’t reliably tell intent? For example, if a teammate repeatedly misses deadlines or withholds info, when do you (1) keep cooperating to avoid an escalation spiral, (2) “defect” to protect yourself (escalate, document, loop in a manager), or (3) reset the game (change the structure/incentives)? What’s the best move that still encourages long-term cooperation without making you look naive or like a pushover?
